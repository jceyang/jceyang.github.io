As a part of imagiCal, UC Berkeley's award-winning competitive strategy and marketing team, I worked with Airbnb to conduct research and formulate product solutions on pain points in the payment process.

Along the way, I learned about payment pain points, UIUX principles, and—to my surprise—the immense complexity of the psychology behind payment models.

The Ask + Methodology

We were asked to understand the severity of pain points in the payment process and then determine the strategic focus of product solutions.

In order to do this, we split our process into 3 steps:

1. Pain Point Identification
2. Pain Point Diagnosis + Analysis
3. Product Solutions

Pain Point Identification

In order to identify pain points, we conducted intensive research using over 466 survey respondents from 33 states, 25 individual interviews, and 3 focus groups—thereby creating a diverse data set to make our initial hypotheses. We then broadened our understand with an analysis of competitors such as Expedia, Homeaway, and Trip Advisor.

Through our research, we learned that:
1. Security concerns were a key issue with payments, due to a lack of transparency with payment securities.
2. Those who booked on behalf of groups had discomfort paying the full amount upfront, due to lags in reimbursement and reluctance in fronting the cost.

Thus, our 2 main pain points were payment security concerns and discomfort with full payment upfront.

Pain Point Diagnosis + Analysis

Having identified our pain points, we performed deep dives into each respective problem to understand where concerns rooted from and user preferences.

Pain Point 1: Payment Security Concerns — Diagnosis

Through comparative research, we determined that the key difference between Airbnb and its competitors was payment protection branding in the payment process. Competitors used this to market their security policies, while Airbnb lacked any branding at all when it came to payment security.

From our research, we also learned that even a simple statement of security or logo indicating security massively improved confidence in the process.

Pain Point 2: Discomfort with Full Payment Upfront — Diagnosis

Through further user research, we determined that the majority of users who booked on behalf of a group used a form of split payment to divide the cost. For instance, users commonly used Venmo as a primary form of payment splitting/reimbursement.

Product Solutions

Using the information gathered from the identification and diagnosis stages, we developed product solutions and created solution mock-ups.

Pain Point 1: Payment Security Concerns — Product Solution

In order to combat payment security concerns, we proposed revisions to the current payment process UI, by including a statement and logo indicating payment security. This clearly communicate to the customer the transaction process and how their payment is protected to alleviate any security concerns

Pain Point 2: Discomfort with Full Payment Upfront — Product Solution

Knowing that most users usually used a form of payment splitting such as Venmo to cover reimbursements, we developed two different production solutions to alleviate this.

1. Venmo integration with checkout, removing the friction in moving to a different platform to complete reimbursements.
2. Native split payment model, which gives a 24-hour window for friends to pay to pay for the house.
